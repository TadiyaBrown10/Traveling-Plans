# New York City

The Big Apple is a wonderful place full of energy and things to do. It is always a pleasure to visit this city anytime of the year. 

Back in 2025, I took my students to visit the city and it was a great experience.

## Places to Visit

### Times Square
The dazzling crossroads of the world, Times Square is a neon-lit plaza in Midtown Manhattan where Broadway meets 7th Avenue. It's famous for its massive digital billboards, Broadway theaters, street performers, and the iconic New Year's Eve ball drop. Whether day or night, the energy here is unmatched.

### Koreatown
Located along 32nd Street between 5th and 6th Avenues, Koreatown (K-Town) is a compact but vibrant neighborhood packed with Korean restaurants, karaoke bars, and cafes. It's the perfect spot for **Korean BBQ**, where you can grill marinated meats like bulgogi and galbi right at your table, paired with an array of banchan (side dishes). Many spots stay open late into the night.

### Ellis Island
From 1892 to 1954, Ellis Island served as the main gateway for over 12 million immigrants entering the United States. Today, it's home to the National Museum of Immigration, where visitors can trace family histories, explore restored immigration halls, and learn about the American immigrant experience. It's typically visited alongside the Statue of Liberty.

### The MET (Metropolitan Museum of Art)
Perched along Fifth Avenue on the edge of Central Park, The MET is one of the largest and most prestigious art museums in the world. Its collection spans 5,000 years of human creativity, from ancient Egyptian artifacts and European masterpieces to modern American art. Plan for at least half a day — you can't see it all in one visit.

### Central Park
An 843-acre green oasis in the middle of Manhattan, Central Park offers a much-needed escape from the city's hustle. Highlights include Bethesda Terrace, the Bow Bridge, Belvedere Castle, Sheep Meadow, and the Central Park Zoo. It's perfect for walking, biking, boating on the lake, or simply people-watching.

### NYU (New York University)
Founded in 1831, NYU is one of the largest private universities in the United States, with its main campus centered around Washington Square Park in Greenwich Village. Rather than having a traditional gated campus, NYU is woven into the fabric of the city, making the streets of the Village its quad. It's renowned for programs in the arts, business (Stern), film (Tisch), and more.
